01 / Position

Independent Governance Architecture

True institutional independence requires separation of powers. An architect cannot objectively design a building if they are also paid to supply the materials.

Our office operates with structural neutrality. We sit on the client's side of the table, designing governance frameworks that serve the family's interest, not a bank's sales quota.

02 / Materials

The Jurisdictional Palette

We view global jurisdictions not as locations, but as architectural materials. Each possesses unique properties of strength, flexibility, or opacity that we combine to build a resilient structure.

Liechtenstein

The Bedrock

Used for the Stiftung. It provides the immutability and non-beneficial ownership characteristics required for asset isolation.

Singapore

The Framework

Used for Operational Holdings and VCCs. It offers territorial tax neutrality, treaty access, and legal speed.

Switzerland

The Vault

Used for custody arrangements and physical storage through licensed third-party institutions. Established standards of banking privacy and security protocols.

Delaware / Cayman

The Conduit

Used for specific structuring use cases where flexibility and pass-through tax status are the priority.
